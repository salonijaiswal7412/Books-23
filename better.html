Better than the Movies

AUTHOR:LYNN PAINTER

Perpetual daydreamer Liz Buxbaum gave her heart to Michael a long time ago. But her cool, aloof forever crush never really saw her before he moved away. Now that he’s back in town, Liz will do whatever it takes to get on his radar—and maybe snag him as a prom date—even befriend Wes Bennet. The annoyingly attractive next-door neighbor might seem like a prime candidate for romantic comedy fantasies, but Wes has only been a pain in Liz’s butt since they were kids. Pranks involving frogs and decapitated lawn gnomes do not a potential boyfriend make. Yet, somehow, Wes and Michael are hitting it off, which means Wes is Liz’s in. But as Liz and Wes scheme to get Liz noticed by Michael so she can have her magical prom moment, she’s shocked to discover that she likes being around Wes. And as they continue to grow closer, she must reexamine everything she thought she knew about love—and rethink her own ideas of what Happily Ever After should look like.

RATINGS AND REVIEWS

GOODREADS:4.4/5
GOOGLE:4.7/5

Better Than The Movies. One of the very best books I’ve read before. I am a romance seeker and I live vicariously through romance books. This is the perfect book to do just that. This book displays a good buildup of two characters. And potential love interest for Wes and Liz. Wes and Liz are strangers, to friends, to best friends and ….more? Liz was longing to be with Micheal with the help of Wes. Wes wants Liz to be happy but Liz begins to enjoy the comfort of Wes. This book is amazing and I will never forget it. I have even started recommending it to my friends.

MY REVIEW

OMGG!!!I loved this book so much.....even though I am not a romance junkie but then also I love every bit of this book.What makes a romance movie great is a good buildup, not too slow and not too rushed. Lynn perfected that part! There is slight foreshadow and romance among characters that allow you to stick to the end. I love the references to movies and songs, and how Liz likes to connect them with situations and people throughout the book. The romance isn’t the only crucial part, since it also captures Liz’s struggles with a loss and trying to find out the future and fix the present.
